About

I'm a second-year PhD student major in Statistics at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. My past research experience focuses on machine learning, with an emphasis on sequential data processing. I'm also interested in factor analysis and network analysis. Currently I am working with Professor Karl Rohe and Professor Sebastian Raschka.

Prior to that, I graduated from Zhejiang University with a B.S. in Statistics and the University of Wisconsin-Madison with a M.S. in Statistics. My full CV can be found here.

Projects

Generating Amino Acid Sequence for Protein Synthesis
Collaborative research project (Prof. Raschka and Prof. Romero)
This project focuses on synthesizing protein from a certain family using deep learning models and could be potentially used to design protein with desired properties. To this end, I implement the Char-RNN and the transformer model using PyTorch.


Model Selection under Spiked Covariance Model
Project under the supervision of Prof. Karl Rohe.
Under the spiked covariance model, we aim at selecting a reasonable number of principal components via the framework of hypothesis testing.


Tweet Sentiment Analysis
Deep learning course project under the supervision of Prof. Sebastian Raschka.
We build 3-class sentiment classifiers using 1.6 million tweets and compare different deep learning methods with traditional machine learning model using criteria of training speed and prediction accuracy.


Yelp Business Analysis
Data science course project under the supervision of Prof. Hyunseung Kang.
In this project, we use the data provided by the Yelp, which includes reviews, tips, business and user information, to provide actionable suggestions for business owners. My main contribution to this project is the score model for review which combines TF-IDF score and user weight. I also implemented LDA and NMF for topic models.
